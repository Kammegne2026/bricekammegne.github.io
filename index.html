Biography

Kammegne Tcheugam Brice is currently a PhD researcher in Theoretical Chemistry at the University of Sussex, supervised by Prof. Hazel Cox. His doctoral research, funded by an EPSRC studentship, focuses on the development of tensor-structured low-rank approximation methods for solving the Schrödinger equation for few-particle quantum systems with high numerical accuracy.

He holds a Master's degree (Distinction) from the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS), Cameroon, and a Master 2 in Applied Mathematics with Highest Honours from École Normale Supérieure de Maroua. His background spans pure mathematics, functional analysis, and computational quantum chemistry.

Brice has undertaken visiting research programmes at the University of Padova (Italy), the University of Dresden (Germany), and the National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (Taiwan), and has presented his work at international conferences across Europe and North America.
